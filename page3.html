The Event Horizon

The "event horizon" is the boundary around a black hole in which nothing, not even light will be able to escape. Basically if you fall far enough into the event horizon of a black hole, there is no turning back and you are pretty much done for.

The Photon Sphere

The photon sphere is a boundary outside of the event horizon where you can basically "orbit" a black hole. In the photon sphere you will not be sucked in, but you also would not have much luck getting out. Theoretically, you can see the back of your own head in the Photon sphere because light would travel around the black hole right back to your eyes.

The Singularity

The singularity is the region in which the gravitational force is basically infinite, this region is usually located in the very center of the black hole. The singularity is the point in which a black hole's force is the greatest. However, whether there is a singularity has yet to be assured.

Accretion Disks

Accretion disks are basically swirls of mass that is getting sucked into a black hole, since light can not escape from a black hole, you can't see any mass get sucked into the black hole.
