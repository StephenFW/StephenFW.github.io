More information on black holes.

How do black holes form?

A black hole forms when a certain volume is compressed into an extreme amount of pressure. When an object is compressed, it will become a black hole when it is compressed smaller than a certain unit. This unit is called the "Schwarzchild Radius", where basically if anything no matter what size or mass is compressed into its Schwarzchild Radius it will become a black hole.

A black hole usually forms when a star reaches the end of its life and collapses in on itself. When a star collapses into the Schwarzchild radius, it will become a black hole.

As a star collapses, its pressure and mass is so great that if a star is big enough, it may collapse into a black hole instead of exploding into a supernova.

Life cycle of a star

  • For regular stars.
    1. A star is formed through the processes that take place in a stellar nebula.
    2. Over time, the star gets larger and larger and becomes a "red giant".
    3. The red giant bursts into a planetary nebula that creates heavier elements that would not have been created with fusion.
    4. A white dwarf remains after the star has collapsed within its own gravitational force.

  • For massive stars.
    1. A massive star is formed through the processes that take place in a stellar nebula.
    2. Over time, the star gets larger and larger and becomes a "red supergiant".
    3. The red supergiant explodes in a supernova.
    4. Either a black hole or a neutron star forms after the supernova due to the star collapsing by its own gravity.
